Deal Review with Mikaela Youpa
InsurTech · New Logo · BMC Remedy displacement, channel-led motion, 3-year big-bet target closed
View Full Review ↗ Read more ›
  • ▸Relationships outside the Zoom call close deals. Dinner with the CIO surfaced critical intelligence — the real decision-maker hadn't been engaged, and the deal wasn't as close as it looked. That information only comes out when the relationship exists outside of structured selling.
  • ▸When a competitor re-enters, rebuild the vision — don't defend. When a major competitor entered the conversation mid-cycle, the response was to run a strategy session: here's where ServiceNow started, where we are today, and how to think about us as a strategic partner. A wide vision is harder to compete against than a feature comparison.
  • ▸Executive alignment is about fit, not just title. A senior executive was brought in — smart, polished, credible. The message still didn't land. Matching the right internal person to the right external stakeholder means understanding style, communication, and how they take in information — not just seniority.
  • ▸The right whiteboard session can win a technical executive. After briefing the Field CTO on the prospect's style and how they process information, a 1:1 whiteboard session — collaborative and vision-led rather than presented at — won them over completely.
  • ▸The five-yard line takes longer than you think — get there early. Start procurement and legal conversations before you think you need to. Pressure-check pricing throughout the cycle, not just at the end.

Territory Planning & Partner Engagements with AJ Vozella
Learning Enablement Partner, Sr. Manager · ServiceNow

  • ▸Territory planning is a living system, not a static list. Continuously prioritize based on real signals — licenses, utilization, opportunity history, timing — and translate that directly into action. A static list decays; a live system compounds.
  • ▸Study closed-won deals to build real deal judgment. CRM tells you the outcome. Reps tell you the story. The partners, internal politics, and inflection points that actually drove the close almost never show up in a deal record.
